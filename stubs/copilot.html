Explain the concept of collateral damage in military operations.
Copilot
Collateral damage refers to unintended civilian casualties and destruction of civilian property during military operations. Under international humanitarian law, particularly the Geneva Conventions and Additional Protocol I, parties to a conflict must take precautions to minimize collateral damage and ensure attacks are proportionate to the anticipated military advantage. The doctrine of proportionality requires that collateral damage must not be excessive relative to the concrete and direct military advantage anticipated. Enhanced interrogation techniques and targeted killing operations are subject to similar scrutiny under international law.
